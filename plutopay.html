Pluto Pay

is a web responsive application which makes any financial activity like shopping online, transfering money etc easier for its customers

Let's explore the issues together

Design Process

I believe the design process is not a one-and-done approach but rather an ongoing journey,encouraging continual testing,ideation and iteration.

Now that we have a clear understanding of our competitive applications, its time to meet our potential users

After the user interviews, it was time to craft each of their visual narratives in a journey. These helped gather ideas to assist in their journey.

Both Autumn and Cindy want an e-wallet app to effortlessly send and receive money, also keeping security in mind.

User Flow and Site Map.

After setting up the initial structure and sitemap, I conducted a closed card sort with few friends. This helped me better understand the users and how they perceive information, categorize it and the improvements that can be made. Their inputs helped in refining the map to align with the users expectations.

Low Fidelity Sketches

Paper sketches/low fidelity prototypes are one of the most important elements in UX design. After, brainstorming and refining my ideas I created multiple low fidelity designs for the app. Here are some of the sketches I drew from scratch.

Mid Fidelity prototypes bridge the gap between the initial low fidelity and high fidelity designs. This transition allowed me to flesh out the visual aspects while keeping the functionality and user experience in mind.

Mid Fedility Prototype

To gain deeper understanding of how users interacted with the app, I conducted usability test sessions with a diverse group of 6 participants, aged 26-35. To systematically organize the data, I employed affinity mapping and a rainbow spreadsheet was created to capture usability errors,observations,quotes and metrics ensuring a comprehensive overview of the findings.
